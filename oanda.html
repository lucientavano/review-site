Account Validation
The questionnaire was standard, no weird question, just the usual regulatory things. The design of that part of the app was a bit too basic to my test (it's the first interaction with the user, more effort could be put into it) and not 100% thought for mobile ("Drag my file"?). But it wasn't buggy, it worked fine, and the back office validated documents very quickly. At this point though, I was far from overwhelmed with the graphic environment I was diving into.
Funding Process
The funding process was effective and fast. I would say it looked even a bit better than the account questionnaire, so I was starting to feel a bit more interested in this app. To my surprise, there was no minimum deposit. Yop, you read that right, you can deposit €1 if you want. You can even verify here. However, on mobile, you can only deposit via credit card, while you can use other methods, including PayPal, on the web version (unfair!). The amount was credited to my balance immediatly and I could start trading right away.
Withdrawals
A very bad point is that I couldn't withdraw from the app directly. The option is not provided (!). You have to go to the website to make a request. But Oanda does process requests, no problem (see screenshots), they can have the verified badge. I could even withdraw my €100 welcome bonus after I met the conditions (initial deposit €200 or more, no minimum volume to trade, only to wait for one month), I had never seen this with any other broker. So I initially deposited €210 in April 2016, then traded a bit to test some strategies, and overall lost around €30. Thanks to the bonus though, I could withdraw €200 + €71.23, total €271.23! I don't think that bonus will still exist at the time you read these lines, but just to say that Oanda is really trustworthy.
Trading Experience
The app is very complex, to say the least. If you are a beginner, honestly, forget it. If you are, however, an experienced trader, you will find the app to be very comprehensive and powerful, despite the low quality of the charts. What is great with Oanda is that there is no minimum trade size. Yes, again, you read that right, you can trade ridiculously low amounts, which makes Oanda a great choice to test strategies in real conditions, since you can risk just a few cents per trade if you like (see my example trade that uses only €1 for margin). That is not common for a forex broker (not comparing with binary options brokers here). Another great feature is the possibility to define default settings for your trades (trade size, stop distance, etc.).
Customer Support
Fast replies, and on point (see screenshots about my enquiry regarding their bonus). However, Oanda is a bit old-fashion, and still does the sales call thing when you open your account. I don't know about you, but I don't like to be called, for anything, and especially by brokers (that I just "met"). This sounds like a dodgy practice even if it's not necessarily one on the case of Oanda. Because of the reputation of the industry I think they should approach the onboarding process differently. I definitely put them on the trustworthy side, so I'd like to see them using different practices.
